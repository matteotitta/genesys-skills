# Archive Case Study Interview Questionnaire

**Purpose:** Extract raw transcript content to feed the case study workflow
**Format:** 30-45 minute customer interview
**Interviewer:** Archive Product Marketer

---

## Section 1: Company Context (3-5 mins)

*Goal: Establish segment, team structure, and program maturity for case study framing*

1. **Tell me about your company — what do you sell, and who buys it?**

2. **How big is your marketing team, and who specifically handles influencer/creator relationships?**
   - *Probe:* Is this their full-time role, or do they wear multiple hats?

3. **Roughly how many creators are you working with today?**
   - *Probe:* Paid partnerships vs. gifting vs. organic fans

---

## Section 2: The Before State (8-10 mins)

*Goal: Document the pain, old workflows, and breaking point*

4. **Take me back to before you started using Archive. Walk me through how you tracked creator content.**
   - *Probe:* What tools were you using?
   - *Probe:* How much time did this take each week?

5. **What was falling through the cracks with that process?**
   - *Probe:* Missed posts? Stories disappearing? Outdated spreadsheets?

6. **Was there a specific moment or event that made you realize you needed a different solution?**
   - *Looking for:* The "breaking point" or buying trigger

7. **Before Archive, how did you prove the ROI of your creator program to leadership?**
   - *Probe:* What metrics were you reporting? How confident were you in those numbers?

8. **Had you tried any other platforms or tools before Archive?**
   - *Probe:* What didn't work about them?

---

## Section 3: Discovery & Evaluation (5-7 mins)

*Goal: Understand competitive positioning and decision drivers*

9. **How did you first hear about Archive?**

10. **What other tools or platforms did you evaluate alongside Archive?**
    - *Probe:* What made you choose Archive over those alternatives?

11. **What was the most important capability you needed from a tool like this?**

12. **Was there anything that almost stopped you from buying?**
    - *Looking for:* Objections that matter to similar prospects

---

## Section 4: Implementation & Usage (7-10 mins)

*Goal: Document specific features used and daily workflow*

13. **Walk me through how you use Archive in a typical week.**
    - *Probe:* Which features do you use most?

14. **What Archive feature has made the biggest difference for your team?**
    - *Looking for:* Specific feature tied to outcome

15. **How has Archive changed the way you find new creators to work with?**
    - *Probe:* Creator Search, competitive intelligence, social listening?

16. **Tell me about your reporting process now. What do you share with leadership?**
    - *Probe:* Are they impressed? What metrics land?

17. **How many people on your team use Archive?**
    - *Probe:* Any collaboration benefits?

---

## Section 5: Results & Metrics (8-10 mins)

*Goal: Quantify outcomes — this is the heart of the case study*

18. **Since implementing Archive, how has the volume of creator content you capture changed?**
    - *Probe for specifics:* Before vs. after numbers, percentages

19. **How much time do you estimate your team saves each week or month using Archive?**
    - *Probe:* What do you do with that time now?

20. **Have you seen any impact on campaign performance or engagement?**
    - *Probe for numbers:* Conversion rates, engagement rates, revenue influenced

21. **If you had to put a number on it, how many full-time employees would you need to do what Archive does for you?**
    - *Looking for:* FTE equivalency metric

22. **What metrics would you point to if someone asked "Is Archive worth it?"**

---

## Section 6: The Signature Moment (3-5 mins)

*Goal: Find the memorable story beat that makes this case study shareable*

23. **Has there been a moment where Archive surprised you — caught something you would have missed, or surfaced something unexpected?**
    - *Looking for:* Taylor Swift-style discoveries, viral moments, celebrity mentions

24. **What's the most exciting or unexpected creator mention you've discovered through Archive?**

25. **Is there a story you tell other people when recommending Archive?**

---

## Section 7: Advice & Endorsement (3-5 mins)

*Goal: Generate quotable content and advice for prospects*

26. **If you were talking to another [job title] at a similar-sized brand, what would you tell them about Archive?**

27. **What would you say to someone who thinks they can manage their creator program manually?**

28. **Would you be comfortable being a reference customer for brands evaluating Archive?**
    - *Probe:* Comfortable with name/title/photo on case study?

---

## Closing

29. **Anything else you'd want people to know about working with Archive?**

30. **Who else on your team might have additional perspective I should talk to?**

## Interviewer Notes

### Quote Capture Priority

Listen for and capture verbatim:
- Pain quotes: "I was spending X hours on..."
- Turning point quotes: "The moment I knew we needed..."
- Solution quotes: "Now with Archive, I can..."
- Results quotes: "We've seen X improvement in..."
- Recommendation quotes: "I tell everyone that..."

### Metric Capture Checklist

| Metric Type | Captured? | Value |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Content volume increase | ☐ | |
| Time saved (hours/week or month) | ☐ | |
| Team size/FTE equivalency | ☐ | |
| Creator program scale | ☐ | |
| Campaign performance lift | ☐ | |
| Revenue or conversion impact | ☐ | |

### Segment Classification

Based on interview, classify customer:
- [ ] SMB Brand (<$50M rev, <50 employees, <100K followers)
- [ ] Mid-Market Brand (≥$50M OR ≥50 employees OR ≥100K followers)
- [ ] Enterprise Brand (≥$1B OR ≥1,000 employees OR ≥5M followers)

### Feature Focus (for case study framing)

Primary features discussed:
- [ ] Social Listening / UGC capture
- [ ] Creator Search / Discovery
- [ ] Campaign Tracking / Gifting
- [ ] Competitive Intelligence
- [ ] Creator Vetting
- [ ] Shoppable UGC Feed
- [ ] Reporting / Analytics
- [ ] Slack Integration
