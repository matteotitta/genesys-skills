## What makes this example effective

### 1. Revenue-backed service breakdown
Instead of just listing services offered, this matrix shows actual FY revenue per service category:

```markdown
| Service Category | # Clients | Total Revenue | % of Consulting | Avg LTV | Avg Months |
|-----------------|-----------|---------------|-----------------|---------|------------|
| **Positioning / Storytelling** | 21 | £412,000 | 53% | £19,619 | 5.2 |
| **Landing Pages + pSEO** | 22 | £676,000 | 87% | £30,727 | 7.1 |
```

This grounds the competitive analysis in actual business performance, not assumptions.

## Key patterns to reuse

### For services businesses
1. Start with your own revenue breakdown by service
2. Map competitors to your service categories
3. Use overlap indicators (High/Medium/Low or ●●●/●●○/●○○)
4. Include pricing comparison with your positioning

### For strategic learnings
1. **Advantages** — Evidence-based (testimonials, data)
2. **Constraints** — Honest assessment with mitigations
3. **Adopt/Skip** — Clear decision framework
4. **DO/DON'T** — Actionable summary

### For solopreneur/boutique models
Focus on:
- Capacity constraints → Service prioritization
- Speed differentiator → vs. agency timelines
- Direct access → vs. junior staff handoffs
- Specialist positioning → vs. full-service agencies
