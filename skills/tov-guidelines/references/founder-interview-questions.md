# Founder Interview Questions

Use these questions to fill gaps identified in the analysis phase. Prioritize questions based on which gaps most impact the guidelines.

---

## When to use this

After Phase 1 analysis, you'll have a list of gaps — things that couldn't be determined from website scraping alone. Use these questions to fill those gaps.

**Interview format options:**
- Live conversation (best for nuance)
- Async written responses (good for busy founders)
- Voice memo (captures natural speech patterns)

---

## Core questions (ask these regardless of gaps)

### 1. Origin and identity

> **If someone asked you "what does [Company] sound like?" in one sentence, what would you say?**

This captures their intuitive sense of voice before analytical questions.

> **What company or person's communication style do you admire? What specifically about it?**

Identifies aspirational voice models.

> **What brand voice makes you cringe? What specifically?**

Identifies anti-patterns from their perspective.

---

### 2. The "I" vs "We" question

> **When you write about [Company], do you think of it as "I" (you personally) or "we" (a team/company)? Has this changed over time?**

Resolves person conflicts if found in analysis.

---

### 3. Audience clarity

> **When you write content, who specifically are you picturing reading it? (Job title, situation, mindset)**

Validates or corrects audience definition.

> **What do you assume they already know? What do you have to explain?**

Determines jargon comfort level.

---

## Gap-specific questions

Use these based on what the analysis couldn't determine:

### If social media voice is unknown

> **Does your voice change on LinkedIn/Twitter compared to your website? How?**

> **Show me a LinkedIn post you were proud of. What made it work?**

### If email voice is unknown

> **How do your marketing emails differ from your website? More casual? More direct?**

> **Can you share an email that felt very "you"?**

### If sales voice is unknown

> **When you're on a sales call, do you sound different than your website? How?**

> **What phrases do you find yourself repeating in sales conversations?**

### If negative scenarios are unknown

> **How do you handle saying "no" or "we can't help with that"? What's your approach?**

> **Have you ever had to fire a client or turn someone away? How did you communicate it?**

### If audience variation is unknown

> **Do you speak differently to [audience A] vs [audience B]? How?**

> **Are there topics you'd discuss with one audience but not another?**

### If formality level is unclear

> **Would you ever use [specific casual phrase] in your content? Why or why not?**

> **Is there a level of casualness that feels "too far" for your brand?**

### If vocabulary choices are unclear

> **Why do you use [term A] instead of [term B]? (e.g., "pipeline" vs "leads")**

> **Are there industry buzzwords you deliberately avoid? Which ones and why?**

### If structural preferences are unclear

> **How long is too long for a paragraph in your content?**

> **Do you have strong feelings about bullet points vs prose?**

---

## Voice calibration exercise

If the analysis found inconsistencies, use this:

> **I'm going to read you two sentences. Tell me which sounds more like [Company]:**
>
> Option A: "[Quote from source 1]"  
> Option B: "[Quote from source 2]"
>
> **Why did you choose that one?**

This resolves conflicts by getting founder's explicit preference.

---

## Anti-pattern validation

> **I noticed you never use [word/phrase] on your website. Is that intentional?**

> **What phrases would you never want associated with [Company]?**

> **If an AI wrote content for you that included "[problematic phrase]", how would you react?**

---

## Future-proofing questions

> **Is your voice evolving? Where do you see it going?**

> **Are there topics or tones you want to start incorporating that aren't on your site yet?**

> **Who else might write content for [Company]? What would they need to know?**

---

## Closing question

> **What haven't I asked that's important to how [Company] sounds?**

Often surfaces constraints or preferences not covered above.

---

## Processing interview responses

After the interview:

1. **Update confidence scores** — Answers that confirm analysis findings → High confidence
2. **Resolve conflicts** — Founder's stated preference becomes the rule
3. **Fill gaps** — Add new findings with "Source: founder interview" attribution
4. **Note contradictions** — If founder says one thing but website shows another, flag for discussion
