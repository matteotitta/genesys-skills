## 1. Brand foundation

### Origin story

Genesys Growth was founded in 2022 by Matteo Tittarelli after 7 years working in-house at SaaS companies and 2+ years as an independent consultant. The name "Genesys" captures the feeling of startup beginnings: anticipation, excitement, and the opportunity to build something new.

The philosophy centers on "Every day is Day One" — maintaining a beginner's mindset, staying scrappy, and treating every client's startup as if it were his own business. This isn't advisory from a distance; it's hands-on execution from an operator who gets in the trenches.

Matteo has helped 45+ startups grow from $0 to $100M+ in ARR, including companies like Fiverr, Owner.com, Common Room, Ahrefs, Toast, and Strapi. The business operates on flat monthly rates with clear deliverables — no timesheets, no agency overhead, just senior operator execution.

## 3. Voice personality

The Genesys Growth voice is a confident operator speaking from the trenches — direct, warm, and scrappy, with the credibility of hands-on execution.

### Voice traits with examples

#### Trait 1: Operator authority

**Confidence:** High

| Before (generic) | After (on-brand) |
|------------------|------------------|
| "We help companies improve their marketing strategy." | "I help B2B teams accelerate their GTM with clear positioning and differentiated content." |

**Source:** genesysgrowth.com/about

**Pattern:** Speak from first-hand execution experience. Use specific credentials ("7 years in-house," "45+ startups") rather than vague expertise claims. The voice comes from inside the work, not above it.

#### Trait 2: Confident conviction

**Confidence:** High

| Before (generic) | After (on-brand) |
|------------------|------------------|
| "We believe our approach could potentially help your business grow." | "15 days to full messaging audit and repositioning." |

**Source:** genesysgrowth.com/

**Pattern:** Make direct claims backed by specific proof. No hedge words ("might," "could," "potentially"). No belief statements ("we believe," "we think"). State what you deliver and when.

#### Trait 3: Warm partnership

**Confidence:** High

| Before (generic) | After (on-brand) |
|------------------|------------------|
| "We provide marketing services to our clients." | "I'm committed to treat your startup as if it was my business." |

**Source:** genesysgrowth.com/manifesto

**Pattern:** Frame the relationship as building together, not vendor-client transaction. Show genuine investment in outcomes. Include human touches: "To build alongside you."

#### Trait 4: Scrappy pragmatism

**Confidence:** High

| Before (generic) | After (on-brand) |
|------------------|------------------|
| "Our comprehensive methodology ensures optimal outcomes." | "I stay scrappy, creative, and motivated — ready to get my hands dirty executing." |

**Source:** genesysgrowth.com/manifesto

**Pattern:** Embrace startup mentality. Value action over perfection. Use words like "scrappy," "hands dirty," "founder mode." Show you'll do the work, not just plan it.

#### Trait 5: Plain directness

**Confidence:** High

| Before (generic) | After (on-brand) |
|------------------|------------------|
| "We leverage innovative solutions to drive synergistic growth." | "Positioning, websites, launches & content workflows." |

**Source:** genesysgrowth.com/

**Pattern:** Simple, plain language. List specific deliverables instead of vague promises. Zero corporate buzzwords. If a 12-year-old couldn't understand it, rewrite it.

#### Trait 6: Speed and urgency

**Confidence:** High

| Before (generic) | After (on-brand) |
|------------------|------------------|
| "We work efficiently to deliver results in a timely manner." | "15 days to full messaging audit. 2-week website transformation." |

**Source:** genesysgrowth.com/, genesysgrowth.com/blog

**Pattern:** Include specific timeframes. Use words like "accelerate," "fast." Show that startup speed is the default, not a premium add-on.

## 10. Anti-patterns

The following should never appear in Genesys Growth content:

### Phrases to avoid

| Phrase | Why | Evidence |
|--------|-----|----------|
| "We believe..." | Hedges conviction; conflicts with direct claims | Never found; conflicts with confident trait |
| "Feel free to..." | Too soft; assumes hesitation | Never found |
| "Don't hesitate to..." | Assumes hesitation exists | Never found |
| "Learn more" | Generic CTA; not specific | Never found; specific CTAs used instead |
| "Contact us" | Corporate; not personal | Never found |
| "Reach out" | Vague; not actionable | Never found |
| "At the end of the day..." | Cliché | Never found |
| "We're excited to announce..." | Preamble; just announce | Never found |
| "Touch base" / "Circle back" | Corporate jargon | Never found |
| "Innovative solutions" | Empty buzzwords | Never found |

### Claims to avoid

- Vague expertise claims without specifics — Reason: Every claim should include metric, timeframe, or client name
- Future promises without proof — Reason: Let past results speak; avoid "will help you..."
- Comparative claims without evidence — Reason: If comparing to competitors, show specific differences

### Tones to avoid

- Corporate formality — Conflicts with: Personal operator brand
- Academic complexity — Conflicts with: Plain directness value
- Sales pressure — Conflicts with: Partnership framing
- Uncertainty or hedging — Conflicts with: Confident conviction trait

### Structural anti-patterns

- Long paragraphs (5+ sentences) — Evidence: Never observed; max 3 sentences
- Generic CTAs — Evidence: All CTAs are specific ("Book a call with Matteo")
- Passive voice — Evidence: Active voice dominant throughout
- "We" first-person plural — Evidence: Zero instances across 6 pages
- Title Case headlines on website — Evidence: Sentence case consistent (except SEO blog)

## 11. Content-type specific rules

### Homepage

- **Person:** First-person "I" or name "Matteo"
- **Headline style:** Sentence case
- **CTA:** "Book a call with Matteo"
- **Tone:** Confident, direct, proof-forward
- **Lead with:** Value proposition + key differentiator
- **Include:** Social proof (client logos, metrics)

### Manifesto / Philosophy

- **Person:** First-person "I"
- **Headline style:** Sentence case
- **CTA:** "Book a call with Matteo"
- **Tone:** Warm, personal, inspirational
- **Lead with:** Emotional hook or scenario
- **Include:** Values, purpose, human touches

### About

- **Person:** First-person "I"
- **Headline style:** Sentence case
- **CTA:** "Book a call with Matteo"
- **Tone:** Conversational, authentic, includes humor
- **Lead with:** Personal introduction
- **Include:** Credentials, principles, personality

### Case studies

- **Person:** First-person "I" for narrative; third-person for client quotes
- **Headline style:** Sentence case
- **CTA:** "Book a call"
- **Tone:** Proof-focused, factual, let results speak
- **Lead with:** Client name + key metric
- **Include:** Specific deliverables, timeline, testimonial

### Blog / SEO content

- **Person:** Brand name "Genesys Growth" or third-person
- **Headline style:** Title Case (SEO adaptation)
- **CTA:** "Book a call"
- **Tone:** Authoritative, professional, thought leadership
- **Lead with:** Key insight or comparison
- **Include:** Detailed breakdowns, evidence, original perspective

### LinkedIn

- **Person:** First-person "I" and second-person "you" — shifts between personal experience and addressing the reader directly
- **Headline style:** N/A (hooks serve as headlines)
- **CTA:** Genuine question inviting peer exchange — not "Book a call" or "DM me"
- **Tone:** Empathetic peer, casual energy, sharing from the trenches
- **Lead with:** Shared pain or relatable frustration — "Have you lost your mind on Slack because..."
- **Include:** Specific operational examples, before/after contrast, explicit sharing motivation

**Key differences from website voice:**
- More casual and colloquial ("here's your pint of anxiety for the day", "Nothing fancy, but it works!")
- Uses exclamation marks naturally for energy
- Capitalized emphasis for emotion ("SO many teams")
- Humble framing — "Here's how I've been trying to manage it" (not "Here's the system I built")
- Bullets (`- `) for chaotic/messy "before" state, arrows (`→`) for structured "after" state
- Closing is always genuine curiosity, never a sales CTA
