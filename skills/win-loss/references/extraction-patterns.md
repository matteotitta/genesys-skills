# Extraction patterns: Signal dictionary

Use these patterns to identify win/loss signals in sales call transcripts.

---

## Product signals

### Feature fit

**Win signals:**
- "This is exactly what we've been looking for"
- "Finally, something that does [specific thing]"
- "[Feature] is the main reason we're choosing you"
- "We love that you have [specific capability]"
- "This solves our biggest problem with [workflow]"

**Loss signals:**
- "We need [feature] and you don't have it"
- "Without [capability], this won't work for us"
- "Can you add [feature]? No? That's a dealbreaker"
- "[Competitor] has [feature] that you're missing"
- "This doesn't do [specific thing] we need"

### Technical fit

**Win signals:**
- "It integrates perfectly with [tool we use]"
- "Your API documentation is solid"
- "Security team approved this quickly"
- "Fits right into our existing workflow"
- "Works with our [specific tech stack]"

**Loss signals:**
- "Doesn't integrate with [critical tool]"
- "Our security team has concerns about [issue]"
- "Won't work with our [infrastructure]"
- "API limitations are a problem"
- "SOC 2 / compliance issues"

### Complexity

**Win signals:**
- "This is so intuitive"
- "Our team picked it up quickly"
- "Implementation was smoother than expected"
- "Simple to set up and use"
- "No steep learning curve"

**Loss signals:**
- "This seems too complicated"
- "Our team won't be able to use this"
- "Implementation looks like a nightmare"
- "Too much of a learning curve"
- "We don't have time to figure this out"

## Messaging signals

### Value clarity

**Win signals:**
- "Now I understand why we need this"
- "The value proposition is clear"
- "I can explain this to my team easily"
- "Makes total sense for our use case"
- "The ROI story clicked for me"

**Loss signals:**
- "I still don't understand what this does"
- "What's the actual value here?"
- "I can't explain this to my boss"
- "This sounds like buzzwords"
- "How is this different from [alternative]?"

### Differentiation

**Win signals:**
- "You're the only ones who do [specific thing]"
- "I looked at [competitors] and you stand out because..."
- "Your approach to [problem] is unique"
- "Nobody else offers [specific capability]"
- "This is what sets you apart"

**Loss signals:**
- "This looks the same as [competitor]"
- "What makes you different?"
- "I can get this from [alternative] too"
- "Why should I switch from [incumbent]?"
- "Not seeing the differentiation"

### Persona resonance

**Win signals:**
- "You really understand our challenges"
- "This is built for someone like me"
- "You speak our language"
- "Clearly designed for [our role/industry]"
- "You get what we're dealing with"

**Loss signals:**
- "This isn't built for [our type of company]"
- "Seems designed for bigger/smaller companies"
- "You don't understand our industry"
- "This is for [other persona], not us"
- "Not a fit for our workflow"

## GTM / Sales process signals

### Discovery quality

**Win signals:**
- "You really listened to our needs"
- "The discovery call was thorough"
- "You asked great questions"
- "You understood our situation before the demo"
- "Felt like you knew us already"

**Loss signals:**
- "You didn't understand our problem"
- "The demo wasn't relevant to us"
- "Generic pitch, not customized"
- "You didn't ask about our needs"
- "Felt like a template presentation"

### Demo effectiveness

**Win signals:**
- "The demo sold us"
- "Seeing it in action made the difference"
- "You showed exactly what we needed"
- "The proof of concept was convincing"
- "Demo addressed all our concerns"

**Loss signals:**
- "Demo didn't address our use case"
- "Couldn't see how this works for us"
- "Demo was confusing"
- "Didn't answer our questions"
- "Showed features we don't need"

### Champion enablement

**Win signals:**
- "I can sell this internally"
- "Give me materials to share with my team"
- "I'm your advocate here"
- "Let me talk to [decision-maker] and get back to you"
- "I know exactly how to position this"

**Loss signals:**
- "I don't know how to explain this to leadership"
- "My boss won't go for this"
- "I can't make the case internally"
- "I'm not the decision-maker and can't convince them"
- "Politics here make this hard"

### Timing

**Win signals:**
- "Perfect timing for us"
- "We have budget this quarter"
- "Need to move on this before [deadline]"
- "This is a top priority right now"
- "We're ready to implement immediately"

**Loss signals:**
- "Bad timing for us"
- "Maybe next quarter/year"
- "Reorg happening, can't commit"
- "Budget freeze"
- "Not a priority right now"

## Pricing signals

### Price point

**Win signals:**
- "Price is fair for what we're getting"
- "Good value for the investment"
- "Within our budget"
- "Actually cheaper than we expected"
- "Worth the premium"

**Loss signals:**
- "Too expensive"
- "Over our budget"
- "Can't justify this cost"
- "[Competitor] is cheaper"
- "ROI doesn't make sense at this price"

### Pricing model

**Win signals:**
- "This pricing model works for us"
- "Per-seat/usage-based makes sense"
- "Predictable costs are important to us"
- "Like that we can scale up/down"
- "Flexible pricing"

**Loss signals:**
- "Pricing structure doesn't fit how we work"
- "Per-seat doesn't make sense for us"
- "Too unpredictable"
- "Hidden costs concern us"
- "Would prefer [different model]"

### Negotiation / Flexibility

**Win signals:**
- "Thanks for working with us on price"
- "Appreciate the flexibility"
- "The discount helped"
- "Terms work for us"
- "Contract length is reasonable"

**Loss signals:**
- "No flexibility on pricing"
- "Can't budge on terms"
- "Minimum commitment is too long"
- "Won't negotiate"
- "Rigid contract"

## Competition signals

### Competitor mentions (track which competitors)

**Win against competitor signals:**
- "We looked at [competitor] but chose you because..."
- "You're better than [competitor] at [specific thing]"
- "Switching from [competitor] because..."
- "[Competitor] couldn't do [thing you can do]"
- "You beat [competitor] on [dimension]"

**Loss to competitor signals:**
- "Going with [competitor] instead"
- "[Competitor] has [feature] you don't"
- "[Competitor] is cheaper/easier/faster"
- "Already committed to [competitor]"
- "[Competitor] is the standard in our industry"

### Incumbent / Switching costs

**Win signals:**
- "Worth the effort to switch"
- "Current solution isn't working anyway"
- "Migration support helps"
- "Pain of staying is greater than switching"
- "Ready to move on from [incumbent]"

**Loss signals:**
- "Too much effort to switch"
- "Already invested in [incumbent]"
- "Migration is a barrier"
- "Good enough with what we have"
- "Team is trained on [current tool]"

## Customer context signals

### Urgency

**Win signals:**
- "Need this now"
- "Deadline is [date]"
- "Board/leadership is pushing for this"
- "Compliance requirement by [date]"
- "Critical business need"

**Loss signals:**
- "No rush"
- "Maybe next year"
- "Nice to have, not need to have"
- "Exploring options"
- "Just looking around"

### Budget / Authority

**Win signals:**
- "Budget is approved"
- "I can sign off on this"
- "Finance is on board"
- "Already allocated funds"
- "Decision is mine to make"

**Loss signals:**
- "No budget right now"
- "Need to get approval from..."
- "Budget frozen"
- "Above my authority"
- "CFO won't approve this"

### Organizational readiness

**Win signals:**
- "Team is excited about this"
- "We're ready to implement"
- "Already have buy-in"
- "This fits our digital transformation"
- "Leadership supports this initiative"

**Loss signals:**
- "Team resistant to change"
- "Not mature enough for this"
- "Internal politics"
- "Siloed organization"
- "Change management is hard here"

## Retention / Churn signals (for existing customers)

### Value realization

**Retention signals:**
- "Getting great results"
- "ROI exceeded expectations"
- "Can't live without this now"
- "Critical to our workflow"
- "Team loves it"

**Churn signals:**
- "Not seeing the value"
- "Underutilized"
- "Hard to measure ROI"
- "Team isn't using it"
- "Shelf-ware"

### Support / Success

**Retention signals:**
- "Support is excellent"
- "CSM is great"
- "Problems get resolved quickly"
- "You're always available"
- "Feel supported"

**Churn signals:**
- "Support is slow/unhelpful"
- "Can't get help when needed"
- "Nobody checks in on us"
- "Issues aren't resolved"
- "Feel abandoned"

### Product evolution

**Retention signals:**
- "Love the new features"
- "Product keeps getting better"
- "You listen to feedback"
- "Roadmap looks exciting"
- "You're innovating"

**Churn signals:**
- "Product hasn't evolved"
- "Competitors have passed you by"
- "Feature requests ignored"
- "Stagnant"
- "Falling behind"

## Speaker attribution

Always note who said what:

| Speaker type | Identifiers | Weight |
|--------------|-------------|--------|
| **Prospect/Customer** | "We," "Our team," "I," customer name | Primary — these are the signals that matter |
| **Champion** | Internal advocate, often first name basis | High — champion sentiment predicts outcome |
| **Sales rep** | "We can," "Our product," "Let me show you" | Context — understand what was pitched |
| **Other stakeholders** | "My boss," "[Name] from [dept]" | Context — understand buying committee |

## Pattern aggregation rules

When counting patterns across multiple calls:

| Frequency | Label | Confidence |
|-----------|-------|------------|
| 1 call | "Single mention" | Low |
| 2 calls | "Emerging pattern" | Medium |
| 3-4 calls | "Clear pattern" | High |
| 5+ calls | "Strong pattern" | High |

**Percentage thresholds:**
- >50% of calls = "Dominant factor"
- 25-50% of calls = "Significant factor"
- 10-25% of calls = "Notable factor"
- <10% of calls = "Minor factor"
